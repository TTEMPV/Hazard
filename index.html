Description of the Hazard

A pandemic is an outbreak by infectious disease that occurs over a wide geographical area. that is of high prevalence, generally affecting a significant proportion of the world's population. Pandemics arise from epidemics, which are outbreaks of disease confined to one part of the world.

People at Risk

Risk of severe outcomes is increased in people of all ages with certain underlying medical conditions and in people who are 50 years and older because of their immune system, they could have heat problems, lung/heart disease, diabetes

How People Are Impacted

Pandemics can lead to widespread illness, increased mortality rates, economic disruption, and significant strain on healthcare systems. The ramifications of a pandemic extend far beyond the immediate health crisis, affecting nearly every aspect of society.

National Preparedness Goal

The information about pandemics aligns with the National Preparedness Goal by emphasizing a coordinated, community-wide approach to enhance preparedness, response, and recovery efforts, particularly for vulnerable populations at increased risk.
